# Blameless Culture

Something to keep in mind, it's important to have a "blameless" culture, which basically means you never blame a specific person but you still can say that things are bad about the product etc.

The point of that is to have a lot of honesty around the product so that we always know what can be improved without anyone feeling attacked or devalued.

When a mistake happens, we blame the process (lack of testing, lack of review, lack of a particular type of test), rather than any person.

Sometimes it can seem like blaming when someone is CC'd but I think CC is just a pragmatic way of managing notifications, it is not intended to say "this person is responsible for this issue"
